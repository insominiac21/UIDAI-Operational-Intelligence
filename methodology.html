Strategic Intent

Why the System Exists

National Aadhaar hardware allocation is broadly proportional to state populations, but the real opportunity for efficiency lies at the District Level.

By identifying localized operational pressure and coverage gaps, this system allows administrators to move from "reactive" to "predictive" resource deployment.

Data Validation

Cleaning Logic

  • Standardized Odisha/Puducherry variants.
  • Unified district spacing and syntax across sources.
  • Cross-validated enrolment counts against census baseline.
  • Pruned joining mismatches (Zero-filters).
Model Features

Feature Engineering

  • Youth Share: 0-5 and 5-18 biometric demand.
  • Coverage Gap: Estimated unmet service need.
  • Update Load: Demographic correction pressure.
  • Embedding: (x, y) space for similarity retrieval.
Scoring Logic

Operational Priority Index (OPI)

OPI is a weighted composite score that maps multi-dimensional operational pressure onto a linear 0-100 priority scale.

Segmentation Strategy

Clustering & Archetypes

Comparing KMeans, GMM, and HDBSCAN, the KMeans model was selected for its operational stability. Every district is assigned a permanent Intelligence Archetype (e.g., High-Stress, Youth-Gap) based on its feature centroid.

Deployment Logic

Actionable Outputs

Prioritize: Allocate hardware to high-OPI nodes.
Interpret: Use Archetypes to select intervention types.
Transfer: Discover peers to scale successful pilots.
