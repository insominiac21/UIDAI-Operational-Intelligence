Why the System Exists

State-level allocation is already broadly aligned with population size, so the project does not try to “fix” macro allocation. The real opportunity is district-level prioritisation, where operational pressure, coverage gaps, and youth demand vary sharply.

The final system therefore acts as a decision layer: it identifies where action is most urgent and which districts are operationally similar enough to share intervention strategies.

Data Cleaning & Validation

  • Standardised state names such as Odisha/Orissa and Puducherry/Pondicherry.
  • Unified district spellings, spacing, and formatting variants.
  • Checked merges across enrolment, demographic, and biometric sources.
  • Excluded corrupted rows where zero values came from join mismatches rather than real absence.
  • Tested temporal features, then removed them because the dataset did not provide meaningful month-level variation for the final model.

Feature Construction

  • Youth share captures future demand.
  • Coverage gap captures unmet service need.
  • Update load captures administrative pressure.
  • Biometric stress captures infrastructure strain.
  • x, y embedding is used for visual segmentation and similarity retrieval.

Operational Priority Index (OPI)

OPI is the project’s ranking layer. It combines coverage gap, youth share, and update load into a single score on a 0–100 scale.

Coverage gap gets the highest weight because it reflects direct service shortfall. Youth share captures incoming demand, and update load captures current operational strain. All inputs are normalized before scoring so that no single variable dominates due to scale alone.

Clustering & Segmentation

We compared several clustering approaches, including KMeans, GMM, Agglomerative clustering, and HDBSCAN. HDBSCAN showed strong separation, but KMeans was selected for the final pipeline because every district receives a stable label and the output is easier to explain and deploy.

The final clusters represent operational archetypes such as high-stress districts, youth-gap districts, and high-volume districts. These labels are derived from the dominant pattern of each cluster centroid.

From Analysis to Action

The model is designed to support three decisions:

  • Prioritise districts with the highest OPI.
  • Interpret each district using its cluster archetype.
  • Transfer strategies across similar districts using the embedding space.

Validation Logic

Before any segmentation, we checked whether the existing allocation pattern is already broadly equitable at the state level. The correlation with Census population share showed that the macro allocation is largely proportional. That result strengthens the project: it means the value lies in district-level prioritisation, not in correcting national-level allocation.
