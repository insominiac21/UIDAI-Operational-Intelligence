Technical Audit

Methodology & Logic

Data Normalization Pipeline

To ensure geographical accuracy and join integrity, we implemented a multi-stage normalization pipeline for state and district nomenclature.

State Canonicalization

Mapping raw spelling variants (e.g., "Dadra & Nagar Haveli" vs "D.N.H.") to a single standard to prevent fragmentation of metrics at the national level.

District Join Resolution

Correcting district mismatching between demographic data and operational reports. 147 districts required manual mapping or spelling overrides.

The Intelligence Model

The dashboard is powered by a K-Means Clustering algorithm, selected over density-based alternatives for its stability and interpretability in operational contexts.

Feature Engineering

  • log_enrol: Normalizes massive variance in district sizes.
  • youth_pct: Identifies districts with aging vs. growing demand pools.
  • coverage_gap: Calculated as 1 - (New Enrollments / Total Population).
  • log_bio_stress: Metrics the failure rate of biometric scanners relative to session volume.

Model Comparison Audit

Model selection was based on maximizing the **Silhouette Score** while maintaining a balanced cluster distribution. The final K=6 model provides the best separation between "Stress Anomalies" and "Infrastructure Hubs".

Data Quality Controls

Not all districts are included in the Priority Engine. Transparency regarding data quality is central to our decision-support philosophy.

Exclusion Criteria

Districts marked as data_issue in the master table are excluded from ranking for the following reasons:

  • Zero demographic activity joins following normalization.
  • Anomalous zero-value reports in biometric stress segments.
  • Districts with >90% missing values in core features.
